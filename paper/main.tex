% ABSTRACT
% =====================================================================
\begin{abstract}
Liquid-cooled artificial intelligence (AI) data centers couple a large,
controllable cooling load to megajoules of thermal mass, yet existing
demonstrations of this flexibility stop short of a day-ahead market
commitment with a delivery guarantee: the deliverable depth moves with
humidity, workload, and thermal state, and a commitment that cannot be
honored becomes either a reliability penalty or a silicon-temperature
excursion. We propose ENCORE, an
envelope-certified offering method for grid-interactive liquid cooling.
ENCORE characterizes the set of deliverable (offer, duration) pairs exactly as
a context-dependent polyhedron under joint hotspot and anti-condensation
constraints, with a closed-form weather-coupled virtual-battery equivalent.
Two nested context-conditional disturbance sets robustify the envelope
offline: a safety set that is never traded for revenue, and a delivery set
whose residual risk the settlement prices. A certified fallback policy then
lets a simple real-time controller inherit both guarantees. We prove that any
offer inside the tightened envelope satisfies thermal safety on the safety
set and meets its settled energy obligation at a designer-chosen delivery
confidence. A case study driven end-to-end by measured workload,
weather-forecast, and price records (a 2.0-million-worker GPU-cluster
trace, archived day-ahead weather forecasts, and ten 2024 ERCOT weeks)
validates both clauses out of sample, with zero in-domain violation episodes
across ten held-out weeks replayed under $20$ activation draws. Day-ahead
workload information alone raises the certifiable offer of a hall driven by
the measured GPU-cluster workload from $40.7$ to $74.6$\,kW per MW of IT
load, and the certified participant earns \$$97$/day per MW during a
scarcity week with no added hardware and no workload impact within the
certified domain.
\end{abstract}

\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}
This paper formulated cooling-side flexibility of liquid-cooled AI data
centers as a day-ahead offering problem and developed ENCORE, which certifies
the deliverable (offer, duration) set as a context-dependent polyhedral
envelope and offers from its robustified interior under nested
safety/delivery disturbance sets. The certificate survives from commitment to
real-time control: across ten measured ERCOT weeks, the certified
participant incurred zero violation episodes within its stated domain while
delivery failures remained inside the priced $\epsd$ budget, against $229$
violation day-seeds for the uncertified envelope. Day-ahead workload
information raised the certifiable offer of a hall under a measured
GPU-cluster workload by $83\%$, and scarcity-week value reached \$$97$/day
per MW of IT load with no added hardware. Certified envelopes therefore turn
cooling-side thermal inertia from a demonstrated potential into a market
resource, with safety guaranteed on the certified domain and excursions
beyond it bounded and small.
